# Creating Helpful, Reliable, People-First Content — key points

Source: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content
Fetched: 2026-07-12

## Self-assessment (content quality)
- Original information/research/analysis?
- Substantial, comprehensive coverage?
- Insight beyond the obvious?
- Non-sensational title?
- Would a reader bookmark/recommend/share it?
- Matches quality of printed reference material?

## Expertise indicators
- Clear sourcing + author credentials.
- Factual accuracy, no easily-verifiable errors.
- Written/reviewed by someone who demonstrably knows the topic.

## E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
- **Experience:** demonstrable first-hand knowledge (used the product, did the work).
- **Expertise:** subject depth.
- **Authoritativeness:** recognized as a go-to source.
- **Trust:** most important; the others build toward it. Weighted heavily for
  YMYL topics.
- (Per the starter guide: E-E-A-T is **not** a direct ranking factor — it's a
  lens for the systems, not a dial.)

## Who / How / Why
- **Who:** add author bylines where readers expect them; link to author
  background; make authorship self-evident. Google "strongly encourages" this.
- **How:** explain methodology; disclose substantial automation/AI; show evidence
  (photos, results, process).
- **Why:** serve the audience first. Content made "primarily to attract visits
  from search engines" is the anti-pattern.

## Avoid (search-engine-first signals)
- Lots of content across many topics hoping some ranks.
- Heavy automation across topics; summarizing others without adding value.
- Trending topics without genuine expertise; targeting SEO word counts;
  entering niches without real expertise; backdating content.

## Author attribution
Add accurate bylines + accessible author background pages establishing
credentials and focus.
